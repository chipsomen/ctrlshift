GLOSSARY

SPECTRAL TYPE

A classification system that categorizes stars based on the characteristics of their spectra, which provides information about their temperature, chemical composition, and luminosity.

LUMINOSITY

The total amount of energy emitted by a star per unit of time, often measured relative to the luminosity of the Sun. The sun has exactly one Solar Luminosity (L☉), which is known to be 3.83 × 1026 watts.

TEMPERATURE

The degree of heat of a star, typically measured in Kelvin, which influences its color and spectral characteristics.

SIZE

The physical dimensions of a star, measured in Solar Radius (R☉). Similar to Solar Luminosities, the Sun is exactly one Solar radii, which is known to be 432,288 miles.

LIFESPAN

The duration for which a star exists and remains active, determined by its mass and energy production mechanisms, ranging from millions to billions of years.
